Get Involved

Your field belongs in this conversation. Your contribution belongs here too.

Council Society 2026 is built around the belief that understanding the world requires every discipline at the table. Whatever role you're in — student, researcher, professional, institution — there is a defined place for you inside this structure.

Ways to Join

Four tracks, one shared work

Choose the track that fits your situation. The form below works for all of them.

Domain Circle Member

Student or Researcher

Join a Domain Circle in your field, contribute to real research, and participate in the Integration Forum. Open to students at any level and independent researchers.

  • 5–30 contributors per circle
  • Conduct research from the ground up
  • Participate in Synthesis Forums
  • Publish under your name in our research outputs
  • Access to mentors and cross-field dialogue
Apply Now
Volunteer

Volunteer Contributor

Support the Action & Outreach Layer with writing, translation, design, social media, or field project assistance. No formal research background required — your skills matter.

  • Flexible time commitment
  • Public Communication or Education units
  • Media & Documentation opportunities
  • Named credit on all contributed work
Apply as Volunteer
Institution or NGO

Institutional Partner

Schools, research centres, and NGOs who want to provide feedback, offer data access, or host student workshops. Partnerships are chosen for genuine value, not scale.

  • Co-design research questions
  • Host field project pilots
  • Participate in joint publications
  • Ethics Circle-reviewed partnership terms
Propose Partnership
Funder or Sponsor

Sponsor an Initiative

Support specific research initiatives, workshops, or documentary projects. All funding arrangements are transparent, reviewed by the Ethics Circle, and published in our annual report.

  • Named on funded initiatives
  • No influence on research direction or findings
  • Transparent terms published publicly
  • Impact reports after every initiative

For Academics & Experts

Mentorship is the centre of what we've built.

Each Domain Circle is led by a named academic expert — someone with earned credibility in their field. If you're a professor, senior researcher, or domain expert interested in mentoring a circle, reach out directly.

Mentors don't just review content — they anchor it. Their presence is the difference between a student project and a credible contribution to public knowledge.

Get in Touch as a Mentor
What Mentors Bring
  • ✦
    Field credibility

    Earned institutional trust that no amount of good design can substitute for.

  • ✦
    Research access

    A door into data, peer review, and institutional context that stays closed without them.

  • ✦
    Cross-field accountability

    Named contributors to the Ethics & Integrity Circle's review standard.
