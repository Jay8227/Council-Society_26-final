Individual mentors bring their own institutional credentials, but the society itself operates independently. This independence is essential to our editorial standards and is maintained by the Ethics & Integrity Circle.
No. Domain Circles are open to students at any level, independent researchers, practitioners, and professionals. What matters is a genuine commitment to the field and an openness to cross-disciplinary dialogue. If you're not sure whether you qualify, send us a message — we'll tell you honestly.
It depends on your role. Domain Circle members typically contribute 3–6 hours per week during active research phases. Volunteer contributors have more flexibility and can define their own availability. We don't ask people to overcommit — underpromising and overdelivering is a better foundation than the reverse.
Every public claim passes through the Ethics & Integrity Circle before publication. This circle operates independently from the Domain Circles and the Core Council, reviewing for evidence quality, potential conflicts of interest, and factual accuracy. The standard is simple: every claim must be traceable to evidence we are willing to publish alongside the claim.
No. We do not accept paid placement, sponsored content, or advertising in any form. Sponsorship of specific initiatives is accepted under transparent terms reviewed by the Ethics Circle — but no commercial or political interest shapes a finding before it is published. Sponsorship terms are published in our annual report.
Council Society 2026 was founded in 2026 and operates as a distributed network. Contributors join from many countries, and Domain Circles can operate across geographies. Our administrative registration and primary contact address will be listed in the Year One Report, published at the end of 2026.
We welcome institutional partners who want to co-design research, host workshops, or offer students a connection to the Domain Circles. Partnerships are chosen for genuine value rather than scale — we're not interested in agreements that look good on a letterhead but don't move the work. Reach out at partnerships@councilsociety2026.org to start a conversation.
